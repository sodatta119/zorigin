Privacy Policy

Zap - local file transfer over Wi-Fi

Last updated: [ADD DATE]

Short version: Zap transfers your files directly between your own devices on your local Wi-Fi network. It has no servers, no account, and no analytics. We do not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal data - ever.

What Zap does

Zap turns one device into a small web server on your local network. Another device on the same Wi-Fi connects to it through a browser to send or receive files. Data moves directly between the two devices. Nothing passes through us or any third party.

Data we collect

None. Zap does not collect or process any personal or usage data. There are no accounts, no cloud storage, no tracking, no advertising, and no analytics SDKs. Your files and their contents are never sent to us and are never visible to us.

Permissions and why they’re used

The Android app requests only what it needs to move files on your own network, and uses them entirely on-device:

  • Storage / All files access - to read the files you choose to share and to save files that others send you. You pick the folder.
  • Local network & Internet - to run the local server and let devices on the same Wi-Fi reach it. Zap makes no outbound internet connections.
  • Notifications & foreground service - to keep the transfer server running and show you that it’s active while you share.

Sharing and disclosure

Because no data is collected, none is shared or sold. Files only ever go to the device you are actively transferring to, on your network, at your instruction.

Security

Transfers stay on your local network and never touch the internet. You can optionally require a password before anyone can connect. Large transfers are integrity-checked so a received file matches the original.

Children’s privacy

Zap is a general-purpose utility and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children.
