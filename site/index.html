The problem

Every “easy” option leaves someone out.

Sharing a file across a mixed set of devices is genuinely broken - and the tools that promise to fix it lean on device discovery that home routers love to block.

AirDrop
✗ Apple-only. Nothing for Android or Windows.
Google Quick Share
✗ No macOS support at all.
LocalSend / Quick Share
✗ Rely on mDNS / multicast / BLE discovery - flaky on real Wi-Fi, so devices often just don’t find each other.
Zap
✓ Every platform. No discovery to fail - you open an explicit link or scan a QR.

Why it just works

It removes the part that breaks.

No discovery layer

Zap never relies on mDNS or Bluetooth - the #1 reason the free tools can’t see each other. You point a browser at a URL or scan a QR. That’s the whole reliability edge.

No app on the receiver

The other device opens a link in any browser and it just loads. Nothing to install, nothing to trust - which also means the person you send to discovers Zap on the spot.

Local & private

Files travel directly between your devices on your own Wi-Fi. No cloud hop, no account, no data collected. Turn on a password when you want a gate.

How it works

Three steps, both directions.

01

Run Zap on one device

The phone or computer becomes a tiny local server and shows a http://… link and a QR code.

02

Open the link on the other

Same Wi-Fi, any browser - or scan the QR to skip the password entirely.

03

Send & receive

Drag files or whole folders in, browse and download out. Big transfers resume if Wi-Fi drops and are verified on arrival.
